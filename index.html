Principle

Leave No Trace is arguably our most cherished and tangible principle. Our community respects the environment. We are committed to leaving no physical trace of our activities wherever we gather. We clean up after ourselves and endeavor, whenever possible, to leave such places in a better state than when we found them. Each participant must remove all items they brought with them – including their trash and all the tiny bits of things that humans tend to shed – when they leave the site.

Is this Recyclable?

Currently, in NOLA you can recycle plastic #1 (PETE), plastic #2 (HDPE), clean (not bright colored) paper and cardboard, glass, metal, batteries.

Make sure you thoroughly clean plastic out - one drop of oil and whole batch becomes unrecyclable.

If paper or cardboard is stained with food - it's not longer recyclable, but you can still compost it.

Is this Compostable?

Any organic material is compostable. However, NOLA currently doesn't support extensive composting. If you want to compost at Compost Now locations, make sure your compost bag doesn't have meat, bones, dairy, oil.

If you compost at home or want to start composting in your yard - you can compost everything: from meat to your nail clippings.

Where to Recycle in NOLA

  • City accepts plastic 1 & 2, metal, paper, cardboard, glass etc every Saturday 8am-1pm
  • Glass Recycling at Glass Half Full Nola
  • Shaving blades, various beauty packaging recycled at zero waste store Vintage Green Review
  • Unwearable torn clothes/fabric drop off at local H&M (via program "I:CO")
  • Got ruined sports shoes? Nike takes them via program "Nike Grind" in and turns into various materials
  • Ended up with extra cardboard boxes? U-Haul take them (and gives them) via program "Take A Box, Leave A Box"

Where to Compost in NOLA

  • Various locations organized by Compost Now
  • Learn to compost in your yard
  • Oyster shells collection

Zero Waste in NOLA
